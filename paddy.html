Days Instructions Audio
DAY 1 - 10 1. Prepare the field by plowing and leveling the soil to create a smooth surface for planting.
2. Apply organic matter such as compost or manure to the soil to improve soil fertility.
3. Soak the rice seeds in water overnight to increase their germination rate.
4. Sow the rice seeds directly in the field, either by broadcasting or by drilling in rows.
5. Cover the seeds with soil, and then flood the field with water to a depth of about 2-3 inches.
6. Maintain the water level in the field to keep the soil constantly moist.
DAY 11 - 20 1. Watch for the rice seedlings to emerge from the soil within 7-10 days.
2. Continue to maintain the water level in the field to keep the soil constantly moist.
3. Apply a basal dose of fertilizer such as urea or ammonium phosphate at a rate of 50-60 kg/ha to improve crop growth.
DAY 21 - 40 1. Watch for the rice seedlings to grow to a height of 6-8 inches.
2. Continue to maintain the water level in the field to keep the soil constantly moist.
3. Apply a top dressing of fertilizer such as urea or ammonium sulfate at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha to support plant growth.
DAY 41 - 60 1. Watch for the rice plants to start tillering, or producing additional shoots.
2. Maintain the water level in the field to keep the soil constantly moist.
3. Apply a second top dressing of fertilizer at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha to support plant growth.
4. Monitor the rice plants for pests and diseases, and take appropriate measures to control them if necessary.
DAY 61 - 90 1. Watch for the rice plants to grow to a height of 2-3 feet and to start developing panicles, or the structures that will produce the rice grains.
2. Continue to maintain the water level in the field to keep the soil constantly moist.
3. Apply a third top dressing of fertilizer at a rate of 20-30 kg/ha to support plant growth.
4. Monitor the rice plants for pests and diseases, and take appropriate measures to control them if necessary.
DAY 91 - 120 1. Watch for the rice plants to reach maturity and the grains to turn golden brown.
2. Stop irrigating the field and allow the soil to dry out for a few days before harvesting.
3. Harvest the rice by cutting the plants at the base and collecting them into bundles.
4. Thresh the rice by beating the grain heads against a hard surface to remove the grains.
5. Dry the rice in the sun for a few days to reduce moisture content, and then store it in a dry place.
